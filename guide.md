# Genius Lyric Style Guide for LLMs

This document provides a complete guide for reformatting unformatted or incorrectly formatted lyrics into the official Genius style, designed for an LLM to process and apply.

### 0. Core Principles

* **Accuracy:** The primary goal is to host accurate, clean, and concise lyrics.
* **Source of Truth:** Lyrics should always follow the song's official streaming version.
* **Completeness:** Transcribe the entire song. All repeated sections must be written out in full.
* **Uncertainty:** If a specific word or format is not covered in this guide, make an educated guess based on the existing rules and the context of the lyric.

### I. Structural Formatting

* **Line Breaks:** Break up transcriptions into individual lines as they are sung or rapped.
* **Repetition:** Type out all repeated lyrics in full each time they occur. Do not use notations like `(x2)`.
* **Language Headers:** If a song is in a language other than English, include a header to specify the language.

### II. Standard Spellings & Slang

* **Preferred Spellings:**
    * `I'ma` (not I'mma, Ima, Imma)
    * `'cause` (not cause, cos, cuz; "Cuz" is acceptable for "cousin")
    * `okay` (not ok, O.K.)
    * `'til` (American English), `till` (British English) for "until"
    * `tryna` (not trynna)
    * `ayy` (not aye, ay)
    * `ho` / `hoes` (not hoe, unless referring to the garden tool)
    * `though` (not tho)
    * `ya` (for "you"/"your"), `yah` (for "yeah"/"yes")
    * `y'all` (not ya'll)
    * `skrrt` (not skrt)
    * `Perc'` (not Perk, Perky, Percy, unless "perk" is used in another context)
    * `bougie` (not boujee, boujie)
    * `shawty` or `shorty` (match artist's pronunciation)
    * `lil'` (not lil, li'l)
    * `woah` (not whoa)
    * `dog` (not dawg)
    * `chopper` (not choppa)
    * `oughta` (not oughtta)
    * `naive` (not naïve)
    * `cliché` (not cliche)
    * `alright` (not all right)
    * `a.k.a.` (use `A.K.A.` if it starts a line)
    * `GOAT`, `VIP`, `ASAP` (use `A$AP` for A$AP Rocky/Mob)
    * `cream` (for money), `C.R.E.A.M.` (for the Wu-Tang Clan song)
    * `HAM` (not H.A.M.)
* **Additional Spellings:** `foenem`, `bronem`, `somethin'`, `nothin'`, `lo'` (for location), `switch`/`switchy`/`switchies`, `blick`/`blicky`/`blickies`, `cuz` (for cousin), `T'd`, `doggy`, `slimy`, `high-speed`, `E-way`, `monyun`.

### III. Numbers & Times

* **General Rule:** Spell out numbers (e.g., "five", "fifty-six"). Spell out multiples of 100 as pronounced (e.g., "one hundred", "thirty-five hunnid").
* **Exemptions (Use Digits):**
    * Proper nouns: `Royce da 5'9"`, `Nintendo 64`, `Area 51`
    * Model numbers & Calibers: `G5`, `.380`
    * Years: `1993`, `2014`
    * Common terms: `24/7`
    * Police slang: `5-0`, `12`
    * Money: `24K`, `10K`
* **Times:** Use numerical digits. Use a colon for minutes (`5:30`). Use "a.m." and "p.m.". For on the hour, use `6 o'clock`.

### IV. Punctuation & Symbols

* **Apostrophes:** Use typewriter apostrophes (`'`), not curly ones (`’`). Use for contractions (`ballin'`, `'em`) and pluralizing single letters (`L's`).
* **Quotation Marks:** Use typewriter quotes (`"`), not curly ones (`“` `”`). Use for song titles or direct quotes within lyrics.
* **Question Marks:** End all questions with a single `?`. Do not add to brand names.
* **Exclamation Marks:** Use only a single `!` for a scream like "Ah!". Remove extras. Do not add to brand names.
* **Hyphens:** Use for scatting (`Ski-bi-dibby-dib`) and stutters (`D-d-d-d-don't`).
* **Em Dashes:** Use an em dash (`—`) for dropped words or lines.
* **Special Characters:** Do not use ™ or ®. Spell out `&` as "and" and `°` as "degrees" unless part of a brand name (`H&M`, `su:m37°`).
* **Censored Words:** Represent censored words with a series of asterisks (`****`).

### V. Capitalization

* **Always Capitalize:** The first word of every line, proper nouns, religious titles (`God`), and acronyms (`TBH`).
* **Brands:** Use conventional capitalization (`Chanel`, `Adidas`), CamelCase (`iPhone`), or all-caps for acronyms (`GEICO`). Capitalization is optional for genericized brands (`band-aid`).
* **Specific Words:** Capitalize `Earth` (the planet) and cardinal directions (`North`, `South`) only when they designate specific regions.

### VI. Non-Lyrical Content

* **Ad-libs:** Place in parentheses with the first letter capitalized (e.g., `(Yeah)`, `(Ooh)`). Echo/delay effects are not ad-libs.
* **Sound Effects:** Describe non-lyrical sounds within asterisks (e.g., `*Gunshot*`, `*Phone rings*`).
* **Missing/Incomprehensible Lyrics:** Represent with `[?]`.
* **Pronunciation:** Transcribe words as the artist says them, using apostrophes for omitted syllables (`Ballin'`, `Gon'`). If a unique spelling makes the word hard to comprehend, use the standard spelling.
* **Special Vocals:**
    * **Reversed Lyrics:** Type out as they sound (e.g., `ti esrever dna ti pilf...`).
    * **Yodeling/Scatting:** Use dashes between syllables. Alternatively, use `[Yodeling]` or `[Scatting]`.

### VII. Specific Category Spellings

* **Firearms:** `Draco`, `TEC-9`, `MAC-10`, `Glock`, `FN`/`F&N`/`Five-seveN`, `Kel-Tec`, `SIG`, `Ruger`, `Smith & Wesson`. Calibers use digits (`.45`, `5.56`). Magazine round counts are spelled out (`thirty-round mag`).
* **Jewelry:** `plain jane`, `presidential`/`presi`, `bust down`, `Richard Mille`, `Cartier`/`Carti'`, `Rolex`/`Rollie`, `Audemars Piguet`, `Cuban`.
* **Drugs:** `Xanax`/`Xan'`, `Percocet`/`Perc'`, `Roxy`, `Addy`, `Wockhardt`, `Hi-Tech`, `Tussionex`, `Actavis`, `zaza`/`za`, `Backwood`/`'Wood`, `molly`, `fetty`.
* **Vehicles:** `straight-eight`, `Redeye`, `392`, `Bimmer`, `Lamborghini`/`Lambo'`, `Durango`/`'Rango`, `Corvette`/`'Vette`, `Maybach`/`'Bach`, `Hemi`, `Scat Pack`, `Trackhawk`, `stolo`.
